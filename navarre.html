Navarre

History

Navarre, FL, traces its heritage back to the early days of Spanish colonization when explorers first arrived on its picturesque shores in the 16th century. Originally inhabited by Native American tribes, the area gained significance under Spanish rule and was named in homage to a province in Spain. Over time, Navarre evolved through various influences, from British presence to its integration into the United States, shaping its identity into the vibrant coastal community it is today.

    Food

    Navarre's culinary scene celebrates the bounty of the Gulf Coast, prominently featuring fresh seafood in its diverse offerings. From succulent shrimp to flaky grouper, local restaurants highlight Gulf-caught treasures in dishes like seafood boils and classic fish tacos. Beyond seafood, Navarre's dining experience encompasses a fusion of flavors, blending Southern comfort cuisine with international influences, inviting visitors to relish a diverse culinary journey by the waterfront.

    Attractions

    Navarre, FL, boasts an array of attractions that cater to nature enthusiasts and history buffs alike. Its pristine white sandy beaches, like Navarre Beach, allure visitors with their tranquility and stunning Gulf views, perfect for relaxation and water activities. The area also features historical sites such as the Navarre Beach Marine Park and the Navarre Beach Sea Turtle Conservation Center, offering a blend of natural beauty and educational experiences for those exploring this charming coastal town.

    Windjammers on the Pier

    Windjammers on the Pier in Navarre, FL, is a beloved waterfront eatery renowned for its scenic views and delicious seafood offerings. Situated right on Navarre Beach Pier, this restaurant offers a relaxed atmosphere where diners can enjoy Gulf Coast specialties like fresh fish tacos and shrimp baskets while overlooking the picturesque waters. With its casual vibe and stunning location, Windjammers on the Pier provides a quintessential dining experience that captures the essence of Navarre's coastal charm.
